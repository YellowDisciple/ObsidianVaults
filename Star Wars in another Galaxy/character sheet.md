###### Attributes
There are 6 Attributes on a character sheet ranging from 7 to 12. They are Athletics, Physique, Will, Intelligence, Empathy and Presence.

###### Skills
There are 17 Skills of wich a subsection will be chosen by each character to have on their character sheet ranging from 0 (not on the sheet) to 4 (this limit can be overcome during gameplay). The Skills are as follows Archeotec*, Bartering, Computers, Contacts, Craft, Deceive, Fight, Force*, Investigate, Kinesthesia, Lore, Notice, Pilot, Provoke, Rapport, Shoot and Stealth. Skills marked with as asterisk (\*) are only usable if they have at least one skill point invested in them.

For character creation all Attributes start at 7 and a player can freely distribute 14 points between the attributes. Only one Attribute may attain the rank of 12. All other attributes must be below 12.

The skills are ranked according to the Fate skill pyramid (Fate Core p.46). One skill at 4, two at 3, three at 2 and four at 1.

A character sheet has 5 aspects according to the fate core rulebook pages 32 and onwards. These are equivalent with Values from the 2d20 SRD and can be invoked with Fate points for a reroll or to set a d20 to 1 before a roll.

[[Stunts]] are the Equivalent of Talents in the 2d20 SRD. They modify a Skill roll in a unique way that distinguishes the character from others in the world. A character starts with 3 Stunts and 3 refresh and may buy up to two extra stunts for 1 refresh each.

Extras are the equivalent of Items. They can be bought with the Games currency [[Money]] and are therefore unbound from the Stunts/Refresh system.

Each character starts their journey with a number Extras and ᖬ as is sensible for their backstory. Talk it out with your GM.

Stunt vs. Extra: When is a piece of equipment an Extra and when ist ist a Stunt. Generally, if it let's you use a different Skill or Attribute than is usual it's a stunt with the Equipment name. The equipment is then so specialized that if another picks it up, they will not get the same benefit out of the equipment.
If it's simpler like +1 Protection or a Blaster with 2 Damage, something that modifies how you move or gives you a slightly modified attack, then it's an Extra and can be bought with ᖬ.

###### Stress tracks
There are two stress tracks. The Mental stress track and the physical stress track. The stress tracks consist of two to four boxes with consecutively bigger numbers. The first box is worth 1 shift, the second worth 2 shifts, the third worth 3 shifts and the fourth worth 4 shifts.
When a character would take shifts of damage as part of an Attack or environmental event, they need to check the box with the corresponding number. If that box is already checked they can check the next higher box. If they cannot check a box they can take a [[character sheet#Consequences|Consequence]] to reduce the number of shifts the Attack has generated or they are taken out of the battle. *Example: Dekar Maw was hit by a force lightning strike and is taking three shifts of physical damage. They could check their 3 shift Stress box on the physical track but it was already checked during an earlier attack this fight. They look to their 4 Stress box which is still free. They check the box and continue the fight.*

Every character has at least the 1 and 2 Stress boxes. 
If they have 8 to 9 in Physique they get the 3 Stress box for the physical stress track and if they have 10 to 11 in Physique they get the 4 stress box for the physical stress track. If they have 12 physique they get one additional Mild Consequence that can only be used for physical injuries.
If they have 8 to 9 Will they get the 3 Stress box for the Mental Stress track and if they have 10 to 11 in Will they get the 4 stress box for the Mental Stress Track. If they have 12 Will they get one additional Mild Consequence that can only be used for Mental injuries.
###### Consequences
There are three Consequence fields on a character sheet, Mild, Moderate and Severe, each worth an increasing amount of shifts. Mild is worth 2 shifts. Moderate is worth 4 shifts and Severe is worth 6 Shifts.
When a character takes more Shifts of damage than they can absorb with a Stress box, they can take a Consequence and reduce the number of shifts the attack generates by the value of the consequence. 
A consequence is a special type of Aspect that marks a received injury. The character who inflicted the Consequence gets a free invocation on the Consequence It needs to be treated after the fight and takes a while to heal. Read Fate Core book on getting rid of Consequences.
*Example Dekar Maw is still in the fight after taking that force lightning. The next round he is hit by a light saber for 4 shifts of physical damage. His 4 Stress box on the physical track is already crossed out and there is no higher box. If he cannot absorb the hit he is taken out of the fight. He looks at his Consequences. He doesn't have any Consequences yet. He Writes **Singed left Arm** into the minor consequences field which is worth two shifts. The reduces the incoming attack by two shifts. His physical 2 Stress Box is still empty and he checks off the box and stays in the fight, albeit with a **Singed left arm** on which his opponent gets a free invoke.*


###### Focuses
Each character starts the game with 6 Focuses. These are specializations that increase the Critical range of a [[skill test]] if they are narratively applicable. A focus is a Word that is narrower that a Skill. For example 'Blaster Pistols' as a focus in the Shoot skill. A Focus usually has an obvious Skill that it's associated with but can be used with any skill. A character with the focus 'Blaster Pistol' that uses their Craft skill to modify or repair the blaster also profits from their extensive knowledge on blaster pistols.
