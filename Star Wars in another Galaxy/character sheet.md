###### Attributes
There are 6 Attributes on a character sheet ranging from 7 to 12. They are Athletics, Physique, Will, Intelligence, Empathy and Presence.

###### Skills
There are 17 Skills of wich a subsection will be chosen by each character to have on their character sheet ranging from 0 (not on the sheet) to 4 (this limit can be overcome during gameplay). The Skills are as follows Archeotec*, Bartering, Burglary, Contacts, Craft, Deceive, Fight, Force*, Investigate, Kinesthesia, Lore, Notice, Pilot, Provoke, Rapport, Shoot and Stealth. Skills marked with as asterisk (\*) are only usable if they have at least one skill point invested in them.

For character creation all Attributes start at 7 and a player can freely distribute 14 points between the attributes. Only one Attribute may attain the rank of 12. All other attributes must be below 12.

The skills are ranked according to the Fate skill pyramid (Fate Core p.46). One skill at 4, two at 3, three at 2 and four at 1.

A character sheet has 5 aspects according to the fate core rulebook pages 32 and onwards. These are equivalent with Values from the 2d20 SRD and can be invoked with Fate points for a reroll or to set a d20 to 1 before a roll.

[[Stunts]] are the Equivalent of Talents in the 2d20 SRD. They modify a Skill roll in a unique way that distinguishes the character from others in the world. A character starts with 3 Stunts and 3 refresh and may buy up to two extra stunts for 1 refresh each.

Extras are the equivalent of Items. They can be bought with the Games currency [[Credits ᖬ]] and are therefore unbound from the Stunts/Refresh system.

Each character starts their journey with a number Extras and ᖬ as is sensible for their backstory. Talk it out with your GM.

Stunt vs. Extra: When is a piece of equipment an Extra and when ist ist a Stunt. Generally, if it let's you use a different Skill or Attribute than is usual it's a stunt with the Equipment name. The equipment is then so specialized that if another picks it up, they will not get the same benefit out of the equipment.
If it's simpler like +1 Protection or a Blaster with 2 Damage, something that modifies how you move or gives you a slightly modified attack, then it's an Extra and can be bought with ᖬ.

###### Focuses
Each character starts the game with 6 Focuses. These are specializations that increase the Critical range of a [[skill test]] if they are narratively applicable. A focus is a Word that is narrower that a Skill. For example 'Blaster Pistols' as a focus in the Shoot skill. A Focus usually has an obvious Skill that it's associated with but can be used with any skill. A character with the focus 'Blaster Pistol' that uses their Craft skill to modify or repair the blaster also profits from their extensive knowledge on blaster pistols.
