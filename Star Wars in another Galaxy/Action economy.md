Like every Pen and Paper RPG, when the Action gets going, we need to slice time into manageable bits. This section describes this Homebrew rulesets Rounds, Turns and its Action economy.

###### Round
A Round is a variable amount of time that organizes the game into manageable chunks. Its length is dependent on the situation. In a high stakes Lightsaber duel a Round might signify a few seconds while in a capital ship combat a Round might be several minutes of exchanged broadsides.
Characters and other entities take Turns during a round. Each Character or other entity usually only get's one turn each round and then passes on to another Character or entity, for them to have their turn.
If multiple parties or factions are involved then, when a character of one faction finishes their Turn, they decide which faction goes next. A faction cannot be given a turn again, until all factions have had a turn. The player Characters faction usually goes first, unless there are circumstances where another faction would conceivably go first, like an ambush, or the GM [[Meta currencies#Threat Spends|seizes the initiative]]. When all characters of a faction have had a turn, that faction cannot be given a turn again this round and are passed over. Once all characters or entities have taken a Turn, the Round ends and a new Round begins. The player Characters go first unless the GM seizes the initiative again.
The Turn order of the player characters is decided on collaboratively by the Players. The Turn order of the non player Characters is decided by the GM.
###### Turn
Once it's a [[Character|Character's]] Turn, they get to perform at least one Minor and one Major Action in whatever order they want. Additional Actions may be [[Meta currencies#Momentum Spends|bought for Momentum]] (Player Characters) or [[Meta currencies#Threat Spends|Threat]] (GM Characters). No matter what, a Character can never perform more than two Major actions in a **Round**. Depending on the length of the turn they can also communicate a reasonable amount of Information to another character, without using an action. Talk to your GM about the timeframe of a Trun in this Action scene and what amount of information they would consider 'a reasonable amount'.

###### Action
There are two categories of actions Major Actions and Minor Actions:
###### Major Action
A Major Action is an Action that takes a considerable amount of concentration or coordination. The Major Actions are:
- Attacking - is a Major action and uses the [[Skill test#Attack|Attack]] Skill test. The Attack Action is used whenever someone or something actively tries to harm another. If the target can react they may use a [[Skill test#Defend|Defend]] skill test against the Attack. *Examples are, Shooting a Blaster, swinging a Lightsaber at someone, firing a ship's weapon system*
- Overcome - is a Major action and uses the [[Skill test#Overcome|Overcome]] Skill test. The Overcome Action is the general catch all action that comes into play whenever a character faces an obstacle that needs to be overcome, but is not actively fighting someone. If someone is in a position to hinder an Overcome Action, they may roll an [[Skill test#Active opposition|Active opposition]] Skill test. *Examples are, Piloting a ship through an asteroid belt, finding a Merchant that sells the part you need in a crowded market, repairing the ships drive core*
- Create an Advantage - is a Major action that uses the [[Skill test#Create an Advantage|Create an Advantage]] Skill test. The Create an Advantage Action is used whenever a Character tries to create a new Aspect in the scene or whishes to create a free invoke on a currently existing aspect. If the target of the Action is something with agency and they can react to the Action, they may use a [[Skill test#Defend|Defend]] skill test against the creation of the Aspect. *Examples are, throwing over the shelves in the shop to create an Aspect "Cluttered Floor" that makes it more difficult for pursuers to catch up, scanning an opposing ship for a weakness in its shield matrix to create an Aspect "Shield Weakness", tripping an opponent in combat to create the Aspect "Unbalanced"*
- Do nothing.
###### Minor Action
A minor action is an action that takes considerably less concentration than a major action. The Minor Actions are:
- Taking aim - When you take Aim, on your next attack this Turn, you may reroll a single d20 or gain one shift of damage if the Attack is successful. You must declare which Bonus you take before making the attack.
- Moving a zone when unhindered - moving more than a zone or moving a zone while there is some kind of obstruction, requires an Overcome Action
- Communicating a more complex amount of Information
- Hunker down - When you hunker down behind some cover attacks by you and against you increase by in in their difficulty. This is only possible while in cover.
- Draw Item - You pick up an item within reach of you or draw an item you are carrying. If using the item doesn’t require a Skill test to use, you can use it immediately as part of this minor action.
- Interact - You Interact with an object in the environment, such as opening a door by pressing the control panel or issuing a simple voice command to a computer. Complex interactions may require an Overcome action and a Skill test instead.
- Stand/Drop prone - You drop to the ground, making yourself a smaller target giving yourself the Aspect "Prone", or stand up from being prone, removing the Aspect "Prone". You cannot Stand and Drop Prone in the same turn.
- Do nothing
