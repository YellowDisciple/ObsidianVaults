An Aspect can be many different things but it's always true. It is a description. A character has their 5 character Aspects, including the high concept and the trouble. A scene can have aspects that describe the central things that are important to that scene. For example 'Raging torrent' or 'Darkness' for a scene during a monsoon or in a dark cave. Or both for a scene in a monsoon at night in the forest. This could also have 'uneven ground' as the roots of the forest make treading there treacherous.

A consequence as part of a conflict is also an Aspect. As are complications (rolling a 20 as part of a [[Skill test]]).

An Aspect can always be the target of an [[invoke]] or a [[compel]].

On a skill test, if an Aspect would narratively benefit or hinder the skill test, then it grants a +1 or -1 difficulty modifier to the skill test.

###### Free invocation
When an Aspect is created by a Character, for example through [[Skill test#Create an Advantage|Create an Advantage]] or by spending two Momentum after a successful Skill test, the Aspect has a Free invocation on it that can be used by that Characters Faction.
A Free invocation can be used to reroll a dice roll in a Skill test or to add one shift to the result of the dice roll, before the outcome is settled.

###### Boost
A Boost is a fleeting aspect with one free invocation. Once invoked the Boost disappears. As other Aspects Boosts are only relevant as long as they are true. Since they are fleeting in nature a shift in circumstances can make a Boost obsolete. So use them quickly.

###### Removing an Aspect
Some Aspects can be removed without a Skill test. If there is no hindrance to remove the Aspect a Character can just remove it. But if there is some resistance by the Aspect to its removal then attempt an [[Skill test#Overcome|Overcome Skill test]]. The usual modifications to [[Skill test#Difficulty|Difficulty]] or [[Skill test#Is my Skill test possible at all?|possibility]] of the Task apply. This usually results in a Difficulty of at least 2 since the base difficulty of an Overcome action is one, modified by at least the Aspect that is being attempted to remove.
