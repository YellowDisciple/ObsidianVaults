There are five different types of basic skill tests
[[Skill test#Overcome|Overcome]]
[[Skill test#Active opposition|Active opposition]]
[[Skill test#Create an Advantage|Create an Advantage]]
[[Skill test#Attack|Attack]]
[[Skill test#Defend|Defend]]
Which all consist of a dice roll against a difficulty number with a number of possible parameters added to the roll, wich will be explained later on this page.
###### The Dice roll
To perform a Skill test you need to roll 2 to 5 20 sided dice (d20). Usually written like this: 2d20 for two dice, 3d20 for three, all the way up to 5d20 for rolling five dice. The basic number of dice for a Skill test to roll is always 2d20. To roll more dice you must buy these dice with one of two [[Meta currencies]]. By spending [[Meta currencies#Momentum|Momentum]] from the Momentum pool or by giving [[Meta currencies#Threat|Threat]] to the GM. The third die of the roll costs 1 Meta currency, the fourth die of the roll costs 2 Meta currency and the fifth die of the Roll costs 3 Meta currency. For the 4th or 5th die, the Meta currency for that die needs to be the same type of Meta currency. You cannot buy the fourth die by spending 1 Momentum or giving 1 Threat. It must be either two Momentum or two Threat. Same with the fifth die, three Momentum or three Threat.
If you gain a free third die from a source, for example a Stunt, then that takes the place of the die bought for 1 Meta currency. Buying a fourth die still costs 2 and a fifth still costs 3.
###### Target number
To interpret the number on your rolled d20s you need a Target number. Each Skill test has an associated [[character sheet#Attributes|Attribute]] and an associated [[character sheet#Skills|Skill]], abbreviated as Attribute+Skill. Add both numbers together to get your Target number for the Skill test. Any result above the Target number is a failure. Any result below or at the Target number is a success and generates a shift.
*Example:
Your Character has Intelligence 10 and Craft 3 and needs to roll an Intelligence+Craft Skill test. Your Target number for this roll is 13, so any result of 13 or below is a success.*
###### Critical success
If your d20 rolls a 1 that is called a critical success and generates two shifts. If you have a thematically applicable [[character sheet#Focuses|Focus]] to the Skill test then your Critical range increases to that of the Skill rating of the involved skill.
*Example:
Let's expand on the Target number example. Your Intelligence+Craft Skill test is an Overcome action to modify a Thermal Detonator with a timer and you have the Focus Explosives. As a Thermal Detonator is an Explosive the Focus applies. Your Target number is still 13, but since the Craft rating of your Character is 3, your Critical range expands from 1 to 3. If you roll a 1,2 or 3 you roll a critical success and gain two shifts instead of one. for any roll from 4 to 13 you still get a success and one shift as that is below or at your target number*
###### Complication
Rolling a 20 on a d20 is a complication. The GM will apply a detrimental [[Aspect]], with one free invoke for your opposition, to your Character or the Scene defining the nature of the complication.
As with Critical successes, Complications can also have an increased range. The standard Complication range is 1, but the GM may spend a point of Threat to increase the Threat range by 1. Since the Complication range is now 2, where before only a 20 was a complication now a 19 and a 20 will create a Complication for you and your team. Other sources might also increase the Complication range, your GM will let you know. The maximum Complication Range is 5, meaning a roll of 16 to 20 will create a Complication.
###### Difficulty
Now we Have a number of dice to roll and a Target number to beat. What's next? Each Skill test has an associated Difficulty.
Difficulties usually range from 0 - Success is almost guaranteed and you mostly just need to see how well you are doing,
to 5 - Success is unlikely and many resources need to be committed to even the odds.
Difficulties higher than 5 are possible but rare and when they are encountered it's usually a monumental task to tackle them.

The standard difficulty for most Skill tests is 1 or 2 but gets modified by [[Aspect|Aspects]] in the Scene. Aspects that hinder the Skill test increase the difficulty by 1 and Aspects that narratively support the action decrease the difficulty by 1. The GM may decide to skirt this general guideline at their own risk.
*Example:
Vazzu D'Nur wants to use Stealth to sneak through the **Darkness**. The difficulty is 0, because the Aspect of the **Darkness** decreases the standard difficulty of 1 by 1 to 0.
In another scene Vazzu D'Nur wants to sneak again but this time he is surrounded by a field of **Gravel**. Now the difficulty is 2 because the Aspect of the **Gravel** is making it harder to sneak as it creates extra noise.*
For any Skill test, tally all the positive applicable Aspects and all the negative applicable Aspects, count them against each other and apply the result to the overall Difficulty.

So now Vazzu D'Nur know how difficult it is to sneak on a **Gravel** field. What does that mean for us as the players? The difficulty is generally the number of shifts a Skill test needs to generate in order for the skill to not be a failure. Yes that's right. Just getting the same number of shifts as the difficulty is not necessarily a straight success. How many shifts are needed for what kind of success depend on what [[Skill test#The 4 skill tests|type of Skill test]] it is. For all Skill tests, getting less shifts than the difficulty is always a failure and getting more shifts than the difficulty is always a success. On a successful roll every shift over the Difficulty awards one point of [[Meta currencies#Momentum|Momentum]]. Which can be used right away or put into the groups [[Meta currencies#Momentum pool|Momentum pool]]for later use.
###### Rerolling a skill test
If you [[invoke]] an aspect you can reroll the dice pool. That means you can pick any dice you want from your roll, this includes choices such as only rerolling 1 die or rerolling all dice and any number in between, and reroll the chosen dice. Any dice that have been rerolled cannot be rerolled through a second source of rerolls such as a talent or another Aspect invocation.
You can invoke an aspect through a [[Meta currencies#Fate|Fate]] point or if you have a [[Aspect#Free invocation|free invocation]].
###### Assistance
A Character can assist another Character on a Skill test if narratively appropriate. Explain what combination of Attribute+Skill you want to use to help the Main Character. If the GM approves you may assist. To assist you roll a single d20 with the target number of your Attribute+Skill combination. If you have an applicable [[character sheet#Focuses|Focus]] increase your critical range accordingly. 
If the Main character rolls at least 1 success your successes count towards the total. The rules for Complications still apply to your roll and if the Skill test has an increased Complication range it also applies to your roll. 
###### Is my Skill test possible at all?
To determine the answer to this question look at the Aspects that are in play. If any Aspects make a Skill test outright impossible, then they are. This however opens an avenue for a [[compel]]. If an Aspect is compelled then it's treated as a failed Skill roll and narrative consequences unfold, but a Fate point is awarded to the compelled Character.
*Example:
Vazzu D'Nur has been sneaking in the **Darkness** to scout out the location and Tal Odai is standing guard in case something happens. He makes out the silhouette of a Sith trooper in the darkness and wants to shoot at him. But he really can't make enough out to realistically hit the trooper. The GM says "If you take the shot, I'll give you a fare point for it. Tal Odai's player agrees to the deal, takes the Fate point and tries to shoot the Sith trooper. But oh who would have guessed it? It's just too damn **Dark** and the shot goes amiss. Now the Sith trooper and his allies are warned that opposition is abound and will react accordingly. Hopefully Vazzu D'Nur is not in a compromised position!*
###### The 5 Skill tests:
We have already determined when a Skill test is a failure and when it's a success. Now we will look at the five Skill tests in detail and also determine what happens when you roll exactly the difficulty on each type of Skill test.
There are three results for an active Skill test, Failure, Tie, Success and a fourth type for a reactive Skill test, Guaranteed Failure.
For the three active Skill tests [[Skill test#Overcome|Overcome]], [[Skill test#Create an Advantage|Create an Advantage]] and [[Skill test#Attack|Attack]] the following are the triggers for each result:
- Failure - This happens when you roll less successes than the difficulty.
- Tie  - This happens when you roll as many successes as the difficulty.
- Success - This happens when you roll more successes than the difficulty.
For the two reactive Skill tests [[Skill test#Active opposition|Active opposition]] and [[Skill test#Defend|Defend]] the triggers for each result differ as these Skill tests are rolled in response to an action of another Character. They are:
- Guaranteed Failure - This happens when you roll less shifts than the difficulty of the roll.
- Failure - This happens when your opponent rolls more shifts than you. In other words, when your opponent has more successes than the difficulty which you set.
- Tie  - This happens when your opponent rolls as many shifts as you.  In other words, when your opponent has an equal amount of successes as the difficulty you set.
- Success - This happens when your opponent rolls less shifts than you. In other words, when your opponent has less successes than the difficulty which you set.
###### Overcome 
The Overcome action is the Skill test to see if you overcome an obstacle. Hacking into a Computer, Fixing a droid, Jumping over a Chasm, Sweet talking that Bartender into giving you Vital information, Sneaking onto a Death Star, these sorts of things. The classical Skill test.
The basic difficulty of an Overcome Skill Test is 1 or as set by an [[Skill test#Active opposition|Active opposition]] Roll.
The three outcomes of an Overcome Skill test:
- Failure - You fail to gain your goal or you gain your goal but suffer a guaranteed [[Skill test#Complication|Complication]] as if having rolled a 20.
- Tie - You succeed at your goal but at a minor cost. The GM will give a narrative of the minor cost or give the opposition a [[Aspect#Boost|Boost]].
- Success - You succeed at your goal and gain momentum for each shift above the difficulty.
  You can spend 2 Momentum to add a narratively appropriate Aspect, with a free invocation, to the result of the Overcome action.
###### Active opposition
The Active opposition Skill test is not an [[Action economy#Action|Action]] but a reaction to an Overcome Action. Every time someone or something is performing an Overcome action and you could actively hinder that action you can decide to provide Active opposition.
The basic difficulty to supply Active opposition is 0, modified by any aspects in play.
The outcomes are
- Guaranteed Failure - If you roll less successes than the difficulty you fail to defend yourself. The difficulty that you set is 0 but if our opponent also rolls 0 successes on their Attack, the Attack is treated as a successful attack with 1 shift.
- Failure - This happens when your opponent rolls more shifts than you. In other words, when your opponent has more successes than the difficulty which you set.
- Tie  - This happens when your opponent rolls as many shifts as you.  In other words, when your opponent has an equal amount of successes as the difficulty you set.
- Success - This happens when your opponent rolls less shifts than you. In other words, when your opponent has less successes than the difficulty which you set.
###### Create an Advantage
Creating an Advantage is a Skill test that either creates a new [[Aspect]], representing the created Advantage with a free Invoke or one that creates a free Invoke on an Aspect that is currently in play. 
The basic Difficulty of a Create an Advantage Skill test is 2 or as set by the [[Skill test#Defend|Defend]] Skill test, if the Aspect is created on an Entity with agency and the target can roll active opposition and decides to do so. 
The three outcomes of a Create an Advantage Skill test:
- If you have less successes than the difficulty then you fail to gain an advantage or you create the Aspect you were looking to create but it now benefits the opposition. In this case they get a free invoke on the Aspect.
- If you have exactly as many successes as the difficulty then you fail to create an advantage but you gain a [[Aspect#Boost|Boost]] instead. If your target was an aspect already in play then you gain a free invocation on that Aspect instead of creating the Boost.
- If you have more successes than the difficulty, then you succeed at creating an advantage and you gain momentum for each shift above the difficulty.
  You can spend 2 Momentum to add another free invocation to the created Aspect.
###### Attack
Attacking is the main way of defeating an opponent in an aggressive manner. This includes physical attacks with weapons, mental attacks with social abilities or esoteric attacks with the force.
The basic difficulty of an attack is 2 or as set by the [[Skill test#Defend|Defend]] Skill test, if the target can roll active opposition and decides to do so. 
- If you have less successes than the difficulty then you fail to attack your target and cause no harm. If they used the Defend action then you may be open to a counter attack if you failed badly.
- If you have exactly as many successes as the difficulty then you deal your weapons shifts in damage, unless all your weapons shifts get absorbed by armor. Then you gain a [[Aspect#Boost|Boost]] instead.
- If you have more successes than the difficulty, then you succeed and inflict a hit equal to the number of rolled shifts above the difficulty plus your weapons shifts. Your target must buy off these shifts with their appropriate [[character sheet#Stress tracks|Stress track]] and [[character sheet#Consequences|Consequences]], or be taken out of the conflict. You also generate Momentum equal to the amount of rolled shifts over the difficulty. Your weapons bonus shifts do not generate momentum. 
  You may also pay two Momentum to create a Boost signifying a temporary advantage that your attack has generated.

The basic Difficulty of a Defend Skill test is 0. Any shifts generated set the opponents difficulty for their test.
