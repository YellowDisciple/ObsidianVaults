In Fate an invoke of an aspect, either as a free invoke as part of a successful 'create an aspect' roll, or as an invoke by spending a Fate point, lets you reroll the whole dice pool or add +2 to the result.

In 2d20 a reroll of the dice pool is usually invoked by spending a point of determination. In the case of this homebrew mashup of the two systems a point of Fate counts as a point of determination and lets you reroll the dice pool in the 2d20 way.
You pick any dice you want from the dice pool, reroll those, and accept the new result. Each die can only be rerolled once. So if you have a source that let's you reroll a single die and later decide to use a Fate point to reroll the pool, that die cannot be rerolled again.

Similar to the Fate +2 invoke an invoke can also just add 1 success to the roll instead of rerolling the dice.
